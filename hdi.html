Human Development Index(HDI)

About Human Development Index (HDI)

The Human Development Index (HDI) is an index that measures three key dimensions of human development.
A long and healthy life – measured by life expectancy.
Access to education – measured by expected years of schooling of children at school-entry age and mean years of schooling of the adult population.
A decent standard of living – measured by Gross National Income per capita adjusted for the price level of the country.

The HDI is published by the United Nations Development Programme and this data is shown in the time-series chart here. This line chart provides a basic overview of the Human Development Index over the last decades around the world. You can show lines of other countries by clicking the country name in the legend to see the change over time or compare development between countries. You can save the chart in different format by clicking the icon on the top right corner of the chart.

The HDI Index ranges from 0-1, higher values are better.

Data Source: http://hdr.undp.org/en/data
